Brand Name
When it came to creating our brand name, we were quite stumped. We just did not seem to be able to think of something we could all agree upon. At first, we were leaning towards something much easier, ‚ÄúTeam Up‚Äù. Although it was simple and already implied what the website was about, we were just not satisfied with it.

We decided to give the name a second draft, thinking about how we could think of a name that sounds truly unique. We decided to look at Odido, which was based on the Greek word ‚Äúakouein‚Äù, which means ‚Äúto listen‚Äù, for inspiration. After long conversations with each other, we wanted a name that exudes confidence and fun. We looked up the translation for volleyball in Latin. There was no direct translation, but the closest thing was ‚ÄúVolapila,‚Äù which is of ‚Äúvolare‚Äù, meaning to fly, and ‚Äúpila‚Äù, which translates to ball.

We took the first word, ‚Äúvolare‚Äù, and started switching some letters around. When we switched the O and the A, we noticed ‚ÄúValore‚Äù almost sounded like ‚ÄúValor‚Äù, which means great courage in the face of danger. After a bit of playing with that, we eventually changed the V to a B. We liked this since it incorporated the ball aspect of the sport.

As the last step, we decided to add an A at the end. We found that just ‚ÄúBalor‚Äù made it sound strange, and adding the A at the end to change it to ‚ÄúBalora‚Äù, made it sound much better and more inviting.

Brand Logo
Our logo went through many phases as well. Anything from incredibly simple, like having one letter, to more complex designs involving a volleyball radar. However, no matter how much we tried, we just were not able to get it right.

After getting the feedback that our logo should be more playful, we decided to heavily lean into this. Instead of trying to salvage what was already there, we decided to scrap all of our current ideas and start completely from scratch. We tried to think of something playful, and what could be the most playful of everything, and concluded that nothing is more playful than classic cartoons.

We decided to make a cartoon character out of a volleyball and make him our logo. Not only is this logo fun, but it also plays into Gen Z and millennials' love of retro aesthetics. In a world where all companies keep simplifying and simplifying their logos, we believe our logo is a breath of fresh air, which helps it stick out among its competitors, creating a truly unique and memorable logo.

      Testing Report

      Our primary objective was to find how our users like our current interface and how we can improve it, so we can make an easy and simple experience for our target audience.

      What should our users be able to do during the testing protocol?

      • Learn the use of Balora in a matter of seconds.
      • Easily navigate the website (locate and join volleyball matches).
      • Find out the playful and approachable brand tone we strive for.


      Participations

      We tested the protocol on five participants from our target audience. To be eligible, participants had to be international students or young adults (aged 18 to 30) living in the Netherlands and interested in volleyball.

      Setting

      • Depending on the participant's availability, the test was administered in-person on campus in a closed room.
      • The participant will use the Balora website's clickable prototype on a laptop screen.


      Protocol

      After a brief introduction, participants will be informed that they are free to speak out loud and that we are testing the website, not them. Four important activities (such as "Find a volleyball game near you" and "Sign up as a new player") will be required of them.
      We'll encourage participants to share their thoughts while they browse the website using a "think aloud" technique. Key observations will be gathered for usability insights, and sessions will be audio-recorded.
      We'll do a brief feedback interview regarding their experience, preferences, and overall impressions following the tasks.

      Introduction

      Thank you for participating in this user test for Balora, a platform that helps people find and join local volleyball games. We're testing the website to see how clear and user-friendly it is. There are no right or wrong answers here.

      As you go through the tasks, please say your thoughts out loud‚Äîanything you‚Äôre thinking, feeling, or wondering. This helps us understand how users experience the website.

      At the end, we‚Äôll ask you a few short questions about your experience and any feedback you have. This is going to take around 15 to 20 minutes.


      Tasks

      Find out what the main function of Balora is.

      Main Tasks (Think Aloud):

      • Find a volleyball game in your preferred location.
      • Join a volleyball match.
      • Create a volleyball match.


      Additional tasks

      • Sign up or log in as a user.
      • Find information on how Balora works.
      • Navigate back to the homepage after joining or creating a game.
      • Find out how many people have joined the match already.


      Feedback

      • Was anything confusing or unclear while using the website?
      • Were there any points where you didn‚Äôt know what to do next?
      • Did the website feel smooth and easy to use?
      • What did you like the most about using the website?
      • What did you like the least?
      • If you had to describe your experience in three words, what would they be?
      • How likely are you to use Balora to find volleyball games? (Scale of 1‚Äì5)
      • Did the website match your expectations based on the first impression?
      • Did the design feel fun and welcoming to you?
      • What would you improve or change about the website?


      Findings

      After creating our website and getting it running, we tested our website on multiple users to get insight into how our app works and how people like it. The findings were surprising and very insightful.

      The first task we had our users do was to find out what Balora is. This went very well. All of the users were able to identify that Balora is a website that helps people find and create volleyball matches using key visuals and clear options.

      Main tasks:

      • Finding a match
      • Joining a match
      • Creating a match


      While no one failed these tasks, there were some errors. For example, one user misunderstood the term "match" as a romantic match, similar to Tinder. This shows a need for clearer terminology.
      Another user noticed inconsistency in the search bar design between the home page and the matches screen. They suggested keeping them consistent. One issue with the prototype was the login page, which did not work.
      Users found this confusing, but this is expected with prototypes. Another comment was about redundancy: there are two buttons for creating a match ‚Äî one in the matches dropdown and one in the top right corner. A user found this unnecessary.
      When users tried to find out how Balora works, they were able to get to the About Us page, but some were confused due to too much text on the homepage. A suggestion was made to either reduce the text or move it to a separate page.

      However, as previously stated, all tasks were completed without interference from any of the Balora staff. The testers remarked that the design of the website was very straightforward for the most part and very user-friendly, with button placements being where they should be and navigation feeling very natural. They noted they really liked the cartoony aesthetic and thought the colour palette complemented it very well. This combination of colour palette and design gave a very welcoming feeling to the users, which they very much appreciated. One participant noted she liked the gender selection option since it allowed her to create an environment in which she felt safer by being able to select a female-only team.

      Overall Impressions

      Positives

      • Clear and straightforward functionality.
      • Tasks were completed without help.
      • Inviting color scheme and logical placements.
      • Strong character in design.

      Negatives

      • Terminology around "match" was confusing.
      • Inconsistencies between pages (e.g., search bars).
      • Visual aspects (color choices and overuse of cards) could be improved.
      • Too much text in some areas, which could be intimidating.


      Improvements

      Based on the testing findings, the following improvements have been made.

      • Instead of ‚Äòmatches‚Äô, the terminology used throughout the website has been adjusted to ‚Äòvolleyball matches‚Äô to prevent any confusion with romantic matches.
      • We made all the search bars fully consistent.
      • The signature colours of Balora have been implemented in more areas throughout the website. The primary buttons are in the main blue colour that represents our brand and the secondary buttons are coloured red. We added coloured borders around cards with the secondary signature colours (red and yellow) and elements like cartoon hands for swiping and folding functions.
      • Regarding the overuse of cards in the volleyball matches page, we have lowered the number of cards a bit; this way, we create more space, making it easier to navigate the website.


      Two points of feedback drawn from the findings have not been adjusted. The first one stated that the amount of text was too much on the homepage. We reflected on this, but concluded that the text was necessary and instead partially solved it by highlighting the key take-aways in bold to make it easier to read without cutting out on any crucial information. Secondly, one participant commented that a second button to create a match was redundant. However, we consciously chose to keep two buttons instead of one to make the user interface more accessible to different kinds of thought processes when searching for the button.

      Besides the suggestions of the participants, we came up with three points of improvement by reflecting on the website. The first one would be improving the login page. A pop-up message could be added, stating that the message was successfully sent.

      Secondly, it would add to the UX if a page where the users can create a new volleyball match, with accessible user interface patterns like dropdown menus, input boxes, and buttons to customise the elements that make up a match (as displayed on the volleyball matches page) were created.

      Lastly, a function where users get added to the website‚Äôs group chat once they sign up for a volleyball match could add to the feeling of safety. In the chat, team members of the relevant volleyball match would be able to get to know each other a bit better before meeting each other in real life. This aspect could make the users feel more grounded and safe in their decision to spontaneously join a volleyball match.

      Conclusion

      The initial prototype of Balora was received surprisingly well. Most things functioned as expected, and the layout made sense to users. There is still room for improvement, but fortunately most of the improvement points have been identified and are ready to be applied. We view these test results and reflections as a positive step forward in improving and growing our platform.

      Campaign Overview

      In the Netherlands, many international students struggle to find a local sport community, especially for team sports like volleyball. Weekly matches at traditional student clubs can be hard to commit to, and language barriers often make things even more challenging.

      Even though volleyball is a popular, non-contact and accessible sport that brings people together, finding a team to play with without the complications of commitment can feel out of reach when you're far from home or don't know where to start.

      That's where Balora comes in. Balora is the helping hand, offering international students a platform where they can find their local volleyball community. They get the opportunity to create and join spontaneous matches in their area, without the stress of a traditional club and the language pressure. Balora serves as an easy gateway to connect and play volleyball your way, anytime, anywhere.

      Why Balora?

      Balora is a new platform that makes playing volleyball spontaneous, accessible, and social. Your skill level doesn‚Äôt matter‚ÄîBalora connects international students in your area who are eager to play volleyball.       Validation of Assumptions

      Problem Interviews

      In our problem interviews with international students in the Netherlands, we explored the specific challenges they encounter when trying to participate in local volleyball games. We identified important challenges that helped shape our concept and focus on elements of our brand, such as accessibility, connection and community building. A commonly mentioned frustration was the difficulty in finding casual, local games that didn't require a long-term commitment or a high skill level. Many felt excluded from existing volleyball clubs due to these factors, as well as the potential language barrier. A problem that was mentioned often in the interviews was the lack of central platforms where they could easily discover and join spontaneous games in their area.

      Participants expressed a desire for a way to connect with other players without the pressure of tryouts or weekly obligations. This lack of a simple solution often led to feelings of isolation and missed opportunities for social interaction and physical activity. The need for a platform that specifically caters to non-Dutch speakers was also highlighted, emphasizing the importance of inclusivity. These observations highlighted the need for an easy-to-use platform that connects the local volleyball scene with international students looking for informal play. Based on these findings, we improved our value proposition by shifting our focus from building a volleyball platform to creating a community-centered, low-barrier platform tailored to international students. We adjusted our plan to emphasize features that give spontaneous play and English focused.

      Solution Interviews

      Throughout our solution interviews, we presented international students with the concept of Balora, our website platform, and gathered valuable feedback to further refine our business model and website features. Many interviewees were hesitant to join structured clubs but were enthusiastic about the idea of one-click access to casual games. The ability to filter games by both location and skill level was seen as crucial for finding relevant and enjoyable matches. The community-driven aspect of Balora, making connections between players in a team setting, resonated with the desire for social interaction and meeting new people. Trust is a crucial element; consumers said that if there were clearer indicators of skill level and a friendly, non-competitive environment, they would be more likely to participate in games and use the platform. Several interviewees appreciated the focus on international students and the promise of a platform that exceeds language barriers. Features like clear game descriptions, easy RSVP options, and potentially even player ratings were suggested to enhance trust and allow smooth game organization. Practical considerations, such as mobile accessibility and clear location information, were also highlighted. Overall, the interviews confirmed a strong interest in an accessible and inclusive platform like Balora that simplifies the process of finding and joining casual volleyball games, giving a sense of community among international students.
